A common cybercrime in India is UPI fraud, which has increased significantly with the rise of digital payments.
In this type of scam, the victim is usually tricked into approving a payment request or clicking on a fraudulent link.
The scammer may contact the victim pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or an online seller.
Typically, the fraud begins when the victim receives a message or call stating that they will receive money.
The scammer then sends a “collect request” through a UPI app or shares a fake link.
When the victim approves the request or enters their confidential details such as OTP or PIN, money is instantly deducted from their bank account.
Students and elderly people are the most common targets, as they may not fully understand how digital payment systems work.
The consequences of such fraud include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online platforms.
With the increasing use of digital transactions, it is important to stay aware and cautious to avoid falling victim to such scams.
